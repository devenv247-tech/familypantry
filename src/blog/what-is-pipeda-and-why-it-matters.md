# What is PIPEDA and Why It Matters for Your Family's Data

Most Canadians have never heard of PIPEDA. But it's one of the most important laws protecting your family's personal information online.

Here's what it is, what it does, and why you should pay attention to it when choosing apps and services for your family.

## What is PIPEDA?

PIPEDA stands for the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act. It's Canada's federal privacy law that governs how private-sector organizations collect, use, and disclose personal information.

In plain English: it sets the rules for what companies are allowed to do with your data.

## What does PIPEDA require?

Under PIPEDA, organizations must:

- Get your meaningful consent before collecting your personal information
- Only collect information they actually need
- Keep your data secure
- Let you access your own data if you ask for it
- Delete your data if you request it and there's no legal reason to keep it
- Be transparent about what they collect and why

## Why does it matter for families?

When you use an app for your family — meal planning, health tracking, grocery management — you're sharing a lot of personal information. Your family members' ages, health conditions, dietary restrictions, allergens, location, and spending habits can all end up in a company's database.

PIPEDA means Canadian companies have a legal obligation to handle that information responsibly. But not all apps are Canadian — and apps based in other countries don't have to follow PIPEDA.

## What about BC PIPA?

British Columbia has its own provincial privacy law — the Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA). It applies to private-sector organizations operating in BC and is considered substantially similar to PIPEDA. If you're in BC, companies handling your data need to comply with PIPA as well.

## What to look for when choosing a family app

When you're evaluating any app that handles your family's data, ask:

- **Where is the data stored?** Canadian servers means Canadian law applies.
- **Is the company Canadian?** Canadian companies are subject to PIPEDA.
- **Do they share data with third parties?** And if so, who and why?
- **Can you delete your account and data?** This is a right under PIPEDA.
- **Is the privacy policy clear?** If you can't understand it, that's a red flag.
